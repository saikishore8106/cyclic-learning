The Concept

Not a straight line —
a rising spiral.

Traditional learning tries to cover everything once, linearly. Cyclic Learning revisits the same landscape multiple times — but each pass goes deeper. You build a broad mental map first, then sharpen detail with every loop.

🗺️
Wide First, Deep LaterStart with the full terrain. Understand the forest before individual trees.
🔁
Spaced Repetition Built InEvery cycle naturally revisits earlier concepts — fighting the forgetting curve.
🚀
Momentum & ConfidenceQuick wins in Cycle 1 fuel the motivation to go deeper in Cycles 2–5.

The Science

Why Cyclic Learning Works

It's not magic — it's cognitive science applied to skill building.

🧠

Spaced Repetition

Revisiting concepts after intervals dramatically improves long-term memory. Cyclic Learning makes this automatic — every new cycle is a spaced review.

🕸️

Schema Building

Your brain learns by connecting new info to existing frameworks. A wide Cycle 1 creates the schema that makes every future cycle stick faster.

📉

Forgetting Curve

Ebbinghaus showed we forget ~70% within 24 hours without review. Cyclic learning combats this by design — each cycle is a strategic memory refresh.

🎯

Desirable Difficulty

Learning that feels slightly hard but achievable creates stronger memory traces. Each cycle raises the challenge just enough to trigger deep encoding.

Get Started

How to Start Your First Cycle Today

1
Choose Your DomainPick one technology — Python + ML, Web Dev, Data Engineering — and commit to it for the full 5 cycles.
2
Map Out Your Cycle 1 TopicsList every major sub-topic in your domain. Don't study yet — just identify them. This is your exploration map.
3
Set a Lightweight Time BoxDedicate 1–2 hours daily for Cycle 1. Move fast. Spend 2–3 days max per topic. Surface-level is the goal.
4
Log, Don't MemorizeKeep a simple notes file. Jot what you encountered, not what you memorized. You'll deepen it in Cycle 2.
5
After Cycle 1 — Repeat the LoopBegin Cycle 2: skim your Cycle 1 notes for 2 days, then re-approach every topic — one layer deeper.
